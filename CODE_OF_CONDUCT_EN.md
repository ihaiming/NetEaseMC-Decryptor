# Contribution Guidelines

Thanks for your interest in this project! It's a small, independently maintained tool. To keep things productive and positive, here are a few practical ground rules.

## The Golden Rule: Act Like a Reasonable Colleague

-   **Be respectful.** Use welcoming and professional language. We welcome contributors of all experience levels.
-   **Critique ideas, not people.** Disagreements are fine when they’re about code or approach. Personal attacks, insults, or comments on someone’s ability or identity are not.
-   **Be constructive.** Aim to propose solutions, not just point out problems.

## Guidelines for Issues & Pull Requests

These are the channels I, as maintainer, can manage. To have your ideas handled smoothly:

**For Issues:**
-   Be clear about your problem or feature request.
-   For bugs, include detailed steps to reproduce and your setup (browser, OS, etc.).

**For Pull Requests:**
-   **Please discuss first.** For any significant change (adding dependencies, major refactoring, UI overhauls), please open an Issue first. This prevents you from wasting effort on something that might not align with the project's vision.
-   **Respect the project's ethos.** This project is deliberately **zero-dependency, single-file, and vanilla JavaScript**. PRs introducing new external libraries are unlikely to be merged.
-   **Explain your work.** In the PR description, clearly state what you've done and why.

## Unacceptable Behavior

This will likely result in your Issue or PR being closed or deleted:

-   Spam, trolling, or advertising.
-   Insulting, discriminatory, or sexually charged language.
-   Doxxing (publishing others' private information).
-   Malicious contributions (malware, backdoors, etc.).

## A Note from the Maintainer

My goal is to keep the project healthy. I'll do my best to read and respond to every contribution. If a contribution doesn't fit the project's direction or these guidelines, I will close it with a clear explanation.

For anything beyond the scope of this repository (like GitHub account-level issues), I'm afraid I can't help.

If you experience harassment or have concerns, please reach out to me directly via an Issue. I will address it as best I can.

**Thanks for reading, and thank you for contributing.**
